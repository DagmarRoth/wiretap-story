# WIRETAP WASTE — CLASSIFIED

A data-driven scrollytelling narrative on U.S. wiretap spending waste (2022-2024). Dark terminal aesthetic exploring the paradox of rising costs and falling effectiveness.

## The Story

From 2022 to 2024, the U.S. spent $405 million on wiretapping. Only 11.3% produced evidence. The rest—$258 million—was waste.

This project investigates three critical findings:

1. **Cost-Effectiveness Collapse** – Cost per order rose 29.5% while incrimination rate fell from 12.9% to 11.3%
2. **Geographic Concentration** – Six states account for 75% of waste; California alone: $80.4M (31%)
3. **Anatomy of Waste** – 35% personal calls, 18% unreadable, 6% duplicate coverage

## Visualizations

The narrative unfolds through interactive charts and imagery:

1. **Divergence Chart** – Dual-axis line graph showing cost rising while effectiveness falls
2. **State/Federal Distribution** – Bar chart showing waste breakdown by jurisdiction type
3. **Sunburst Chart** – Two-ring hierarchy breaking down waste by category
4. **Full-Bleed Image** – Federal building photography
