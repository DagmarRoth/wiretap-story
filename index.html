A CALL IS PLACED

Two parties connect. The conversation is private — or so they believe. The signal travels through copper wire and fiber, through towers and switches.

02

THE TAP IS APPLIED

A court order is issued. Somewhere in the infrastructure, a device splits the signal. The government joins the call. Unannounced. Invisible. Federally authorized.

03

4,740 TAPS IN 2024

Federal and state courts authorized 4,740 wiretap orders in 2024. Each one costs $234,272 on average. The total: $405 million.

04

$405 MILLION SPENT

That is what the United States spent on wiretapping in 2024 alone. The question is not whether they can afford to listen. It is whether listening is working.

2022: THE BASELINE

Cost per wiretap: $180,847. Incrimination rate: 12.9%. A system that was already expensive and already not working well.

06

2023: COSTS ACCELERATE

Cost jumps to $204,516 — a 13% increase in one year. The incrimination rate edges down to 11.8%. The lines begin to diverge.

07

2024: THE GAP WIDENS

Cost reaches $234,272 — up 29.5% in two years. Incrimination falls to 11.3%. You are paying more to capture less. Every. Single. Year.

SIX STATES. 80% OF WASTE.

Wasted intercepts cluster where federal and state agencies overlap — in high-population jurisdictions where multiple law enforcement bodies operate without coordination.

09

CALIFORNIA: $80.4M

California alone accounts for 31% of national wiretap waste. The FBI, DEA, state police, and local task forces operate in the same cities, tapping the same suspects, billing separately.

10

THE REDUNDANCY MACHINE

When two agencies tap the same suspect independently, each pays full price. The evidence — if any — is filed twice. The waste is billed twice. The suspect's privacy is violated twice.

$258 MILLION. ZERO RESULTS.

This is the documented waste — 2024 alone. More than a quarter-billion dollars in surveillance that led to nothing actionable. Let us break it down.

12

35% — PERSONAL CALLS

$90.3M spent intercepting personal conversations — family calls, social arrangements, the texture of ordinary life. Not criminal. Not relevant. Billed in full.

13

18% — ENCRYPTED DATA

$46.4M spent on content the government could not read. End-to-end encryption defeated the tap entirely — but the invoice went through regardless.

14

6% — DUPLICATE COVERAGE

$15.5M in redundant taps — multiple agencies surveilling the exact same targets simultaneously. The same silence, purchased twice.
